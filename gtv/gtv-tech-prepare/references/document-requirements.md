# GTV Document Requirements — Working Reference

## Verify Before Planning

This reference supports an interview; it is not legal advice or a substitute for current instructions. Check these live sources before treating any requirement as current:

- [Appendix Global Talent](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/immigration-rules/immigration-rules-appendix-global-talent)
- [Digital technology eligibility](https://www.gov.uk/global-talent-digital-technology/eligibility)
- [Documents needed for endorsement](https://www.gov.uk/global-talent-digital-technology/documents-you-need-to-apply-endorsement)
- [Apply for endorsement](https://www.gov.uk/global-talent-digital-technology/apply-for-endorsement)
- Tech Nation's live application guidance, when available

The Immigration Rules define four optional criteria. GOV.UK's simplified eligibility page displays five bullets only because it splits the founder/executive and employee routes within **O1 — Innovation**. Do not map those two routes as separate criteria.

## Current Published Package

| Item | Current GOV.UK rule |
|---|---|
| CV | Typed; career and relevant publication history; up to 3 sides of A4 |
| Recommendation letters | 3 letters; each up to 3 single sides of A4, excluding author credentials and contact details |
| Technology-business connection | Separate proof if the applicant has been a founder or senior executive of a technology business in the last 5 years |
| Criteria evidence | Maximum 10 documents; each up to 3 sides of A4 |

The criteria evidence must include at least:

- 2 documents showing recognition as a leader or potential leader; and
- 4 documents showing any 2 optional criteria, using 2 documents for each.

CV and recommendation letters do not count toward the 10-document evidence cap. The same piece of evidence cannot be used for more than one criterion.

The current public document page does not state a fixed personal-statement length. The live form may ask narrative questions. Inspect the actual form and current Tech Nation guidance before advising on prompts, limits, upload format, file size, naming, or submission mechanics.

## Responsible Authorship

The applicant must write their CV, narrative form responses, and applicant-created evidence text in their own words. Each recommendation-letter author must independently write their own letter. The assistant may extract facts, ask questions, organise an evidence inventory, map evidence, and provide factual planning bullets, but it must not draft or rewrite submitted prose for any author.

Tech Nation has published guidance against AI- or language-tool-created application material. Because its live guide and exact wording can change, verify the current guidance before submission. Do not claim that prose style can prove who or what wrote a document.

## Narrative Form Responses / Personal Statement

First capture the exact current form prompt and limit. Then help the applicant plan facts for the requested topics, which may include:

- who they are and how their digital-technology work developed;
- material achievements and the applicant's personal contribution;
- recognition by others;
- why the UK is relevant to their plans;
- how their work would benefit UK digital technology; and
- future professional and beyond-occupation activity.

Planning quality checks:

- specific facts rather than generic praise;
- metrics with dates, baselines, and sources where available;
- a clear distinction between personal and team/company outcomes;
- UK plans that are true, concrete, and consistent with the applicant's record; and
- consistency with the CV, evidence, recommendation letters, and live form.

Named cities, institutions, accelerators, events, or programmes are not official requirements. Mention only genuine, current plans. Do not invent UK-facing activity to make the response sound tailored.

## CV

### Official Checks

- typed and no more than 3 sides of A4;
- includes career history and relevant publication history; and
- matches the application on material dates, roles, organisations, and claims.

### Planning Guidance

Prioritise attributable impact over generic responsibilities. Useful sections can include achievements, professional experience, products, open source, speaking, publications, and awards when they are relevant and true. This is structuring advice, not a required GOV.UK template.

Do not claim that every date must match LinkedIn because an assessor always checks it. Instead, identify discrepancies across all sources the applicant controls and ask them to resolve or explain those accurately.

## Recommendation Letters

### Official Requirements

Each author must:

- be an established expert in digital technology, as judged by the endorsing body; and
- have known the applicant's work for at least 12 months.

Each letter must:

- be specific to the Global Talent application;
- say how the author knows the applicant;
- show the applicant's achievements and why they demonstrate leadership or potential leadership;
- say how the applicant would benefit from living in the UK;
- say how the applicant would contribute to UK digital technology;
- explain the applicant's future work plans; and
- provide examples different from the other letters.

It must be typed, dated, signed, include the required telephone/email and applicable organisation details, and come with the author's CV or other accepted credentials proof.

### Selection Guidance

Rank a pool before choosing three. Consider:

- the 12-month knowledge requirement as a hard gate;
- direct knowledge of the strongest evidence;
- credible expertise in digital technology;
- ability to give specific, truthful examples;
- recency of the relevant knowledge; and
- whether the three letters collectively provide useful, non-duplicative perspectives.

An internal manager or colleague is not automatically invalid. State the relationship honestly and assess whether the author can provide credible specifics. Independence can strengthen the set, but do not invent a rule that one letter must be external.

The assistant may prepare factual bullets and questions for the applicant to discuss with each author. It must not draft the letter or tell the applicant to ghostwrite it.

## Proof of Connection to a Technology Business

If the applicant has been a founder or senior executive of a technology business in the last 5 years, current GOV.UK guidance requires separate proof. It gives examples such as:

- domestic or international sales showing customer numbers by country; or
- the last audited accounts, signed by directors, with balance sheet and profit-and-loss statement.

If accounts are unaudited, GOV.UK currently says they must cover more than 12 months. Appendix Global Talent also addresses commercially successful established businesses, share ownership, and dissolved businesses. Check both live sources for the exact current package.

Do not turn the examples into an exclusive list or require an invented funding, revenue, or salary threshold.

## Criteria Evidence

### Stable Mapping

| Label | Criterion |
|---|---|
| **Mandatory** | Recognition as exceptional talent or exceptional promise in digital technology in the last 5 years |
| **O1 — Innovation** | Founder/executive innovation in a product-led company, or employee innovation in a new digital field or concept; pathway wording differs |
| **O2 — Work beyond occupation** | Recognition for work outside the applicant's occupation that advances the field |
| **O3 — Significant contributions** | Significant technical, commercial, or entrepreneurial contributions in a product-led company; pathway wording differs |
| **O4 — Academic contributions** | Academic research published or endorsed by an expert, subject to the exact pathway wording |

Read Appendix Global Talent for the exact Talent/Promise differences before final mapping.

### Strength Assessment

For each proposed document, ask:

- What exact criterion does this support?
- What did the applicant personally do?
- What changed as a result, and over what period?
- What evidence supports each material claim?
- Who can corroborate it?
- Can an assessor understand the context without insider knowledge?
- Is the document within the current limit and legible?

Strong evidence is criterion-specific, attributable, concrete, corroborated, significant, and easy to follow. Internal proof is not automatically invalid, and external proof is not automatically strong. Explain the limitations of each source rather than imposing unofficial exclusions.

### One Document, One Criterion

Assign each submitted evidence document to one criterion only. Do not double-count it or duplicate it cosmetically. If the same underlying achievement could support distinct documents, each must contain genuinely different evidence and independently establish its assigned criterion. Treat this as a cautious possibility, not a strategy for filling document slots.

### Criterion-Specific Questions

**O1 — Innovation**

- What was new?
- Which O1 route applies: founder/executive, or employee in a new field or concept?
- Is the company product-led where the wording requires it?
- What was the applicant's role and what outcome followed?

**O2 — Work beyond occupation**

- Is the activity actually beyond the applicant's occupation?
- How did it advance the field?
- What was the applicant's contribution, reach, and outcome?
- What credible records or witnesses support it?

**O3 — Significant contributions**

- Is the organisation a product-led digital technology company?
- What technical, commercial, or entrepreneurial contribution belongs to the applicant?
- Why is it significant rather than ordinary competent work?
- What metrics or corroboration establish that significance?

**O4 — Academic contributions**

- Is the research relevant to digital technology?
- What did the applicant contribute?
- Is it published or endorsed as required by the current pathway wording?
- What credible evidence shows the venue, endorsement, or impact?

Do not impose a particular platform, journal tier, citation count, audience size, funding amount, salary rule, or named programme unless current official guidance does.

## Building Evidence Before Applying

When a criterion is thin, help the applicant identify genuine work they would value independently of the visa: sustained open-source contribution, documented mentoring, collaborative work, research, speaking, product innovation, or measurable company contribution. Then ask:

- Which criterion would the completed work actually support?
- What outcome and corroboration would exist?
- What realistic timeline follows from the work itself?
- Would delaying materially improve the case?

Do not promise that completing a checklist or waiting a fixed number of months will satisfy a criterion. Isolated activity created only to fill an application slot may be weak; genuine relevance, contribution, and evidence matter more than volume.

## Evidence Mapping Checklist

- [ ] Mandatory recognition has at least 2 plausible documents.
- [ ] Each of 2 selected optional criteria has at least 2 plausible documents.
- [ ] No document is assigned to more than one criterion.
- [ ] No cosmetic duplicate is counted as independent evidence.
- [ ] The package contains no more than 10 criteria-evidence documents.
- [ ] Each document is within the current page limit.
- [ ] Claims, dates, names, metrics, and attribution are consistent.
- [ ] Founder/senior-executive business proof is included where required.
- [ ] Live form and submission instructions have been checked.
