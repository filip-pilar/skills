## The Taxonomy: One Mandatory Requirement + Four Optional Criteria

Appendix Global Talent defines **four** optional criteria. GOV.UK's simplified eligibility page shows five bullets because it splits the two routes within innovation — founder/executive and employee in a new field — into separate bullets. They remain alternatives inside one innovation criterion and must not be counted as two independent criteria.

An applicant must:

1. show recognition as exceptional talent or exceptional promise in digital technology in the last 5 years; and
2. provide at least 2 pieces of evidence for each of any 2 optional criteria.

Use these stable labels rather than the obsolete OC1–OC5 numbering:

| Label | Optional criterion |
|---|---|
| **O1 — Innovation** | Founder/executive innovation in a product-led digital technology company, **or** employee innovation in a new digital field or concept |
| **O2 — Work beyond occupation** | Recognition for work outside the applicant's occupation that advances the field |
| **O3 — Significant contributions** | Significant technical, commercial, or entrepreneurial contributions in a product-led digital technology company |
| **O4 — Academic contributions** | Exceptional ability shown through academic research published or endorsed by an expert, subject to the pathway wording below |

## Pathways

| | Exceptional Talent | Exceptional Promise |
|---|---|---|
| Core question | Is the applicant recognised as a leader? | Is the applicant recognised as having the potential to be a leader? |
| Career stage | Established track record | Must be at an early stage in their career |
| Experience | No mechanical years threshold; assess established recognition and track record | GOV.UK says applicants are *likely* to have less than 5 years in technology; a longer career in another field is compatible |
| Settlement | Verify the current leader timeline live | Verify the current potential-leader timeline live |

Do not turn 5 years into a mechanical cutoff. Around that point, assess recognition, career stage, trajectory, and the evidence as a whole.

### Mandatory Recognition Requirement

For Talent, the applicant must be recognised by others as a leading talent in digital technology in the last 5 years. For Promise, they must be recognised by others as a potential talent in that period.

Probe for independently supportable recognition: awards, invited speaking, acknowledged open-source impact, credible press, selective appointments, customer or partner recognition, or other evidence that people beyond the applicant's immediate team recognise the work. These are examples, not mandatory sub-tests. Salary, employer brand, accelerator membership, funding, follower counts, or pedigree can provide context but are not automatic proof.

## The Four Optional Criteria

### O1 — Innovation

**Exceptional Talent wording:** a proven track record for innovation as:

- a founder or senior executive of a product-led digital technology company; or
- an employee working on a new digital field or concept.

**Exceptional Promise wording:** innovation as:

- a founder of a product-led digital technology company; or
- an employee working on a new digital field or concept.

Assess what was actually novel, the applicant's role, and evidence of the resulting impact. Do not impose invented thresholds for funding, revenue, team size, specific technologies, or public citations.

### O2 — Work Beyond the Applicant's Occupation

The work must contribute to advancement of the digital technology field beyond the applicant's occupation. Potential examples include acknowledged open-source work, mentoring, collaborative projects, community leadership, standards work, or speaking.

Assess the applicant's personal contribution, the reach or outcome, corroboration, and whether the activity is genuinely beyond their occupation. Do not assume that a named platform, a star count, a single event, or unpaid status automatically passes or fails the criterion.

### O3 — Significant Contributions

**Exceptional Talent wording:** significant technical, commercial, or entrepreneurial contributions as a founder, senior executive, board member, or employee of a product-led digital technology company.

**Exceptional Promise wording:** significant technical, commercial, or entrepreneurial contributions as a founder or employee of a product-led digital technology company.

Focus on the applicant's attributable contribution and its significance. Useful proof can include product outcomes, adoption, revenue or savings, technical performance, organisational change, customer impact, and credible corroboration. Company success alone does not prove the applicant's contribution.

### O4 — Academic Contributions

For Talent, Appendix Global Talent refers to academic contributions through research **published or endorsed by an expert**. For Promise, the Rules refer to research **endorsed by an expert**. The simplified GOV.UK page describes published or endorsed research for both pathways; apply the current Rules and flag the presentation difference if it matters.

Assess the work's relevance to digital technology, the applicant's contribution, publication or expert endorsement, and credible indicators of impact. Do not require a Google Scholar profile, a particular journal tier, a citation threshold, or a specific academic credential unless current official guidance does.

## Application Evidence Package

Current GOV.UK guidance requires:

| Item | Current published rule |
|---|---|
| CV | Typed, career and relevant publication history, up to 3 sides of A4 |
| Recommendation letters | 3 letters; each up to 3 single sides of A4, excluding the author's credentials and contact details |
| Technology-business connection | Additional proof if the applicant has been a founder or senior executive of a technology business in the last 5 years |
| Criteria evidence | Maximum 10 documents; each up to 3 sides of A4 |

The criteria evidence must include at least:

- 2 documents showing recognition as a leader or potential leader; and
- 4 documents showing any 2 optional criteria, using 2 documents for each.

The same piece of evidence cannot be used for more than one criterion. Do not encourage cosmetic duplication. If distinct documents arise from one underlying achievement, each must contain genuinely different evidence and independently support its assigned criterion; do not assume an assessor will find repackaging persuasive.

The current public document page does not state a fixed personal-statement length. Inspect the live application form before checking a form response against a character limit.

## Recommendation Letters

Each author must be an established expert in digital technology and must have known the applicant's work for at least 12 months. Each letter must:

- be specific to the Global Talent application;
- say how the author knows the applicant;
- show the applicant's achievements and why they demonstrate talent or promise;
- address how the applicant would benefit from living in the UK;
- address how the applicant would contribute to UK digital technology;
- explain the applicant's future work plans; and
- provide examples different from the other letters.

The letter must be typed, dated, signed, include the required contact and organisation details, and come with the author's CV or other accepted credentials proof. Apply the established-expert and 12-month-knowledge gates first, then compare direct knowledge, specificity, and credibility. Treat recency and relationship perspective as context; internal authors are not automatically invalid and external titles are not automatically strong.

## Founder and Senior-Executive Business Evidence

Current GOV.UK guidance requires proof of connection to a technology business for a founder or senior executive in the last 5 years. Examples include sales/customer information or the last audited accounts; if accounts are unaudited, GOV.UK currently says they must cover more than 12 months. Check the live document page for the current wording before submission.

Appendix Global Talent also requires evidence of commercially successful established businesses, share ownership, or dissolved businesses in the last 5 years where the applicant was a founder or senior executive. Do not reduce this to a single financial threshold.

## How to Assess Evidence

Rate evidence against observable qualities:

- **criterion fit** — it directly supports the assigned requirement;
- **attribution** — it distinguishes the applicant's work from team or company outcomes;
- **specificity** — dates, actions, metrics, and context are concrete;
- **corroboration** — credible records or witnesses support material claims;
- **significance** — it shows why the outcome matters in the field or company;
- **legibility and completeness** — the assessor can understand the claim and proof within the allowed document.

External proof can strengthen a case, but do not claim that every fact must be publicly searchable or that internal evidence never counts. Treat anecdotes, alumni patterns, and practitioner heuristics as prompts for scrutiny, not rules.

## Common Risks to Surface

- recognition is asserted but not shown by others;
- an achievement belongs to the company or team, with no clear personal attribution;
- innovation is ordinary delivery with no demonstrated novelty;
- a company is service-led rather than product-led, or the product connection is unclear;
- work-beyond-occupation activity shows participation but not advancement of the field;
- academic evidence does not show relevance, contribution, publication, or expert endorsement;
- recommendation authors lack the required 12-month knowledge or cannot substantiate the core case;
- evidence is reused across criteria, incomplete, illegible, inconsistent, or over the current limit;
- pathway selection treats years of experience as the only test; or
- applicant-authored documents contain generic or templated language the applicant cannot independently stand behind.

## Responsible Authorship Boundary

Do not write paste-ready application prose. The applicant authors their CV, form responses, and applicant-created evidence text; each recommender authors their own letter. Provide assessment, factual inventories, questions, evidence mapping, and planning bullets only. Tech Nation has published guidance about applicant-created material and AI or language-processing tools, but its live link and wording can change. Verify the current guidance before submission rather than claiming a detection method or inferring authorship from style.
