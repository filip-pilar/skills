# Digital Technology Sector — Qualifying Guide

## Start from the Official Test

Use the current [Appendix Global Talent](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/immigration-rules/immigration-rules-appendix-global-talent) and [GOV.UK digital-technology guide](https://www.gov.uk/global-talent-digital-technology), not role-name folklore.

For digital technology endorsement, the Rules currently require:

- a **technical applicant** to show proven technical expertise with current technologies in building, using, deploying, or exploiting a technology stack and building technical infrastructure; or
- a **business applicant** to show proven commercial, investment, or product expertise in building digital products or leading investments in significant digital-product businesses.

Eligibility therefore depends on the work, the organisation, and the evidence together. Neither job title nor company type alone decides the case.

## Usually Straightforward to Position

Examples can include:

- software engineering, machine learning, data science, cybersecurity, cloud, mobile, games, and technical infrastructure;
- technical leadership in building digital products;
- product, commercial, growth, or investment leadership with demonstrable expertise in digital products or significant digital-product businesses; and
- founders whose businesses genuinely build digital products.

These are examples, not an exhaustive eligible-roles list. A senior title without relevant expertise and impact is not enough; a less senior applicant can still have a credible Promise case if the official criteria and evidence are met.

## Roles and Contexts That Need Probing

Ask what the applicant personally built, led, deployed, commercialised, or invested in; whether the organisation is product-led; and how the result is evidenced.

### Consulting, Agencies, Outsourcing, and Contracting

Work that is purely client service or delivery can be difficult to map to optional criteria that expressly require a product-led digital technology company. Do not declare the person categorically ineligible. Explore:

- whether the applicant worked inside or built a genuine digital product rather than only delivering a service;
- whether their own startup, side product, or open-source work provides a stronger basis;
- whether work beyond their occupation advances the field; and
- whether research or other qualifying evidence fits a different criterion.

Do not claim that applicants from named employers never succeed. Assess the facts against the current wording.

### Technology Work in a Non-Technology Organisation

Using technology in another industry is not automatically the same as expertise in the digital technology field. Probe:

- whether the applicant built a digital product or technical infrastructure rather than merely used standard tools;
- whether the work shows the required technical or business expertise;
- whether the relevant optional criterion requires a product-led digital technology company; and
- whether qualifying work outside the main role exists.

### Adjacent and Hybrid Roles

For design, marketing, sales, project management, business analysis, QA, technical writing, procurement, or hybrid creative/technical roles, inspect the substance:

- Is the applicant building or materially shaping a digital product?
- What relevant technical, product, commercial, or investment expertise do they personally demonstrate?
- Is the claimed contribution significant and attributable?
- Does the evidence fit the exact criterion being claimed?

Do not imply that every C-suite or VP role at a technology company qualifies. Equally, do not reject a role because its title is unconventional.

### Hardware and Deep Technology

Clarify the digital component: software, data processing, technical infrastructure, or digital-product integration. If the work is outside the digital-technology route, tell the user to check whether another Global Talent field may be relevant; do not invent a “creator pathway.”

### Early-Career Applicants

Exceptional Promise requires an early-stage career and evidence of potential leadership. GOV.UK says Promise applicants are *likely* to have less than 5 years in technology, but years alone neither qualify nor disqualify them. Probe for achievements, recognition, innovation, significant contributions, work beyond occupation, or academic contributions relative to career stage.

If evidence is thin, offer a genuine evidence-building plan without promising that a fixed number of months or a checklist of activities will produce eligibility.

## Product-Led Check

For the innovation and significant-contribution criteria, ask:

- What digital product does the company build?
- Who uses or buys it?
- Is revenue primarily tied to a repeatable product or to bespoke services and staff time?
- What did the applicant personally contribute?
- What evidence shows novelty or significance?

“Product-led” here describes the kind of digital technology company in the Rules; it is not merely a go-to-market methodology label.

## Work Beyond the Applicant's Occupation

Open source, mentoring, collaborative projects, community activity, standards work, or speaking may support the work-beyond-occupation criterion when they genuinely advance the field. Assess contribution, reach, outcomes, and corroboration. Do not impose unofficial platform, audience, star-count, payment, or selection-process thresholds as hard rules.

## Founder and Senior-Executive Proof

If the applicant has been a founder or senior executive of a technology business in the last 5 years, current GOV.UK guidance requires separate proof of connection to that business. Examples include sales/customer evidence or accounts. Appendix Global Talent also addresses commercially successful established businesses, share ownership, and dissolved businesses. Check the live official wording before advising on the exact package.

## Diagnostic Questions, Not Eligibility Rules

These questions can expose useful evidence but are not official tests:

- Can a credible third party explain the applicant's individual impact?
- Is there a clear before-and-after outcome?
- Does the work matter beyond ordinary competent performance in the role?
- Is the claimed innovation actually new, or an established approach applied competently?
- Would the evidence make sense to an assessor without insider context?

## Geographic and Immigration Context

Applicants can apply from outside or, subject to the current switching rules, from inside the UK. Do not claim that UK-based applicants face an undocumented “inside bias.” Do not imply that an endorsement application protects expiring immigration permission; GOV.UK says endorsement applications and endorsement reviews alone do not extend permission.

Specific UK contribution plans are an official assessment factor, but no city, institution, accelerator, event, or ecosystem name is inherently favoured. The plan should be truthful, relevant to the applicant's record, and current.
