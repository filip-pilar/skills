# Product Argument Quality Gate

Critique and revise with this decision aid before delivery. Do not expose it as
a scorecard or use it as a heading template.

## Thesis and identity

- Define a specific change in reality, concrete audience, and credible reason
  to exist beyond a category, feature bundle, or added AI.
- Take a position strong enough to exclude plausible alternatives.

## Coherence and coverage

- Explain the problem, current inadequacy, jobs, principles, non-goals, ideal
  journey, and minimum major concepts in plain language.
- Show how participants enter, act, receive value, and return. Address public,
  private, user, operator, maintainer, developer, buyer, and agent experiences
  only where they shape the system.
- When present in the vision, explain Codex, Codex Sites, project skills, and
  recurring loops as product capabilities.
- Remove or combine concepts that do not strengthen the thesis.

## Distinctiveness and strategy

- Express differentiation through behavior, incentives, experience, or
  structure, not adjectives or agent terminology.
- Avoid an accidental generic board, roadmap, dashboard, wrapper, or other
  familiar category. Make the broader opportunity follow logically.
- Verify, attribute, or label competitor and market claims as hypotheses.

## Trust, agency, and incentives

- Make proposal, decision, publication, commitment, moderation, override, and
  reversal authority clear.
- Align privacy and public commitments with expectations; preserve meaningful
  human agency without ceremonial approvals.
- Address material gaming, moderation, incentive, and credibility failures.

## Direction and learning

- Make each phase a coherent expression of the enduring thesis and identify the
  riskiest beliefs to learn without reducing the vision to an MVP.
- Tie success to changed behavior or experienced value. Include validation
  questions, material risks, and tensions.

## Decision usefulness

- Make assumptions visible, routine decisions resolved, non-goals protective,
  and remaining founder decisions few and consequential.
- Enable aligned founder, designer, and product-engineer decisions without the
  preceding conversation.
- Remain a product argument, not architecture, schemas, APIs, or backlog.
- On revision preserve valid decisions and remove superseded claims.

Revise any weakness that changes product identity, coherence, trust,
distinctiveness, or direction. Do not lengthen the PRD merely to cover prompts.
